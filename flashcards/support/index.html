Can I draw on my flashcards?

Yes you can! iStudious - Flashcards w/ Handwriting and Rich Text allows you to put whatever text you want on your flashcards as well as draw whatever you want. With our pen and text tools you can change the font to look however you want it to and you can change the pen size and color to allow for intricate drawings. You can also draw on your card while taking a quiz to help you figure out equations or to simply write down helpful hints.

How do I create a new stack of flashcards?

There are 2 ways in which you can create a new stack of flashcards. You can either create a new empty stack that you can then add new flashcards to individually, from the web, or import cards from Quizlet. You can also create a new stack of flashcards directly from a Quizlet import. On the main Stack screen, simply tap the + button and choose which option you would like.

How can I delete one of my flashcard stacks?

On the main flashcard stack screen, either swipe your finger from left to right over the stack you want to delete and tap the Delete button that pops up, or tap the Edit button and delete whichever stacks you would like.

What are Leitner boxes?

Leitner boxes allow you to categorize your flashcards based on difficulty level. That way, you can choose to study just your Hard cards, or just your Easy cards, or all of them at the same time.

When I'm done with a quiz, why does it ask me if I want to update the boxes of my cards?

We've designed it so when you're done with taking a quiz, you can automatically have the difficulty of your cards updated. Any flashcards you got correct will be promoted into the next box (i.e if the card was in the Medium box, it will now be in the Easy box) and any incorrect cards will be demoted (i.e if the card was in the Medium box, it will be moved to the Hard box). You can remove the Prompt to update your cards by going to the Flashcard Settings screen and turning Prompt? to No.

Can I add flashcards from Quizlet.com into one of my existing flashcard stacks?

Yes, you can. Just drill into the stack that you want to add cards to and choose 'Import Flashcards from Quizlet'. This will allow you to add as many flashcards from Quizlet into an existing stack as you'd like.

What does 'New Flashcards From Web' do?

'New Flashcards From Web' allows you search within Google and Wikipedia. You can highlight any text you want and then you're presented with the option to Search Quizlet or create a New Flashcard. If you Search Quizlet it will search all of the quizlet stacks for the term you highlighted. If you choose to create a New Flashcard, the highlighted text will be placed on the back of a new flashcard. You can then enter whatever text you want for the front of the flashcard and draw on the flashcard as you see fit.

What's the deal with the Quizzes??

iStudious - Flashcards has an integrated quizzing system that allows you to study the flashcards you've created. Before you begin the quiz, you have the option to study the cards in order or randomized, all the cards, or just the cards in a particular Leitner box, and you can also choose how many cards you want to be quizzed on, 10, 20, 30, 40, 50 or all of them. To begin the quiz, just tap Begin Quiz and you will be presented with all the cards that match your criteria. Tap anywhere on the card to see the back of the card and tap the red x if you got the question wrong, and the green check if you got the answer right. The statistics update along the top as you progress through the quiz. On any of the questions, you can tap the 'Annotate' button to draw on the front or back of your card. You will need to tap the top of the flashcard in order to flip the card.

How do I edit one of my existing Flashcards?

When you're on the Flashcard Info screen inside one of your stacks, simply tap Edit Flashcard. From there you will see a list of all of your flashcards. This list can be filtered by Leitner box. Find the card you want to edit and tap on it. Once you're on the Flashcard Edit screen, tap the Edit button in the top right hand corner. You can now change the text on the front or back of you card, as well as add handwriting or drawings to your cards. You can also change which Leitner box that card is located in. Tap the Done button when you have completed all of you edits and your changes will be saved.
